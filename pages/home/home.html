PLASTICO2

MAKING SOLUTIONS USING POLLUTION

We are the problem, but we can also be the solution. From the industrial revolution to now days, the concentration of atmospheric CO2, has increased drastically causing the rise of the temperature of Earth.
  1. Our goal is to use the bacteria E. coli to reduce the level of CO2 in the marine environment.
  2. The enzyme carbonic anhydrase will be used to decompose the carbonic acid into CO2 and H2O.
  3. The CO2 will be fixed by the bacteria and will be used for the production of PHBs, useful for bioplastic synthesis.
Much of the CO2 is absorbed by the seas, which causes the creation of carbonic acid, which lowers the pH of the medium.
In the last 20 years the pH has decreased 0.1 units, that is, the acidity of the water has increased by 26%.

This causes

the reduction of aquatic species, so the trophic networks are affected.
limits the calcification of some species, preventing their growth.
damage to coral reefs, causing serious impact on the planet's biodiversity.
